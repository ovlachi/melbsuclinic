The MSUC was founded in 2020 by a small, multidisciplinary team of clinicians (registered psychologists, nurse practitioners, etc.), who have been supporting people with substance dependence over the years.

We offer specialist assessment and counselling for individuals experiencing substance dependence and/or other mental health issues, ranging from mild to moderate.

As a client of the MSUC you have a right to expect that:

  • You will be treated with respect
  • You will receive a clear explanation of the service you will receive
  • Your consent for any service will be sought by the psychologist prior to the service commencing and as it progresses
  • You will receive an explanation about the nature and limits of confidentiality surrounding the service
  • You will be clear about the goals that you and the psychologist are working toward
  • You will receive competent and professional service
  • You will receive a clear statement about fees
  • An estimate of the number of sessions required to achieve your goals will be discussed.

We believe that every symptom (including substance dependence) is the creative (psychological, mental and/or behavioural) “product” of the individual. As such, no one has the right to label them as “wrong”, something that needs to be fixed or corrected, or take them away from the person whom they belong to.

What we can do though is to walk beside you during your self-discovery and self-development, so that you do not have to do it on your own.
