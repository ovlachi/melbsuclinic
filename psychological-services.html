Psychological services

The MSUPC is dedicated to providing high quality and evidence-based psychological services to people experiencing a range of mental health challenges.

Our Psychologists are experienced in providing psychological therapy and treatment for a number of issues including:

  • depression
  • anxiety
  • divorce and family issues
  • trauma/PTSD
  • work-place issues
  • grief and loss
  • relationship and parenting issues

Individual counselling

Your treatment may require one or more assessment/diagnostic sessions. The role of the assessment phase is to help identify both the areas of concern and strengths of an individual, on which subsequent psychological treatments can build on.

    The initial appointment generally also covers the following:

  • Orientation regarding the process of therapy - including fees and treatment agreement
  • Discussion of your current concerns (what has brought you to therapy)
  • Formulation of a treatment plan that suits you and aims to meet your goals.

The purpose of the initial appointment is to collect as much information about your history and social/family context as possible. The interview usually takes up 50 to 60 minutes.

Following the initial appointment selected psychological tests may be administered. (The time involved depends on the selected psychological tests).

Typical sessions are 50 to 60 minutes, although the initial appointment - and some of the sessions, depending on the therapeutic approach used - may be longer.

Fee structure

Please get in touch for the current fee schedule.

Clients need to provide at least 24 hours’ notice to change or cancel an appointment. If no notice is provided, a cancellation fee of $30 will be charged. Payment is accepted in the form of credit/debit card at the end of the session (or by bank transfer in case of online sessions).

Medicare rebates

A referral under the Medicare system entitles you for up to 10 'rebated' counselling sessions per calendar year (please have your GP complete the referral specifically to your allocated clinician at the Melbourne Substance Use and Psychology Clinic).

  • Clients with a Mental Health Plan from a referring psychiatrist or GP are eligible to claim a Medicare rebate, which will reduce the out-of-pocket expense per session
  • There is provision for bulk-billing for those undergoing financial hardship in which case there will be no out of pocket fee (as Medicare will be billed directly)
  • Under the Medicare system, you must have a review with the referrer after the first six sessions to continue receiving the rebate for the final four sessions
  • If treatment extends beyond 10 sessions, the cost will continue to be as agreed per session. For those who were bulk-billed, the cost of sessions will be negotiated on a sliding scale based on current income
  • Please note that bulk-billing spaces are limited; please enquire when the next appointments are available.
